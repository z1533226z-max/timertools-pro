How to Use a 20 Minute Timer

Twenty minutes of daily meditation rewires your brain. Harvard research confirms structural changes in just 8 weeks of consistent 20-minute sessions.

👁

20-20-20 Eye Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Protect your eyes from screen fatigue with this ophthalmologist-recommended habit.

📚

Study Block

A 20-minute focused study block hits the sweet spot for learning without fatigue. Stack three blocks for a full hour with breaks between.

Why 20 Minutes Is the Sweet Spot

Twenty minutes (1,200 seconds) is a scientifically significant duration. It is long enough to enter a state of focused attention but short enough to maintain that focus without fatigue. This makes it the gold standard for meditation, eye care intervals, and micro-learning blocks.

The 20-20-20 rule for eye health is one of the most practical uses for a 20-minute timer. Digital eye strain affects over 50% of screen users, and this simple habit can significantly reduce symptoms like dry eyes, headaches, and blurred vision.
