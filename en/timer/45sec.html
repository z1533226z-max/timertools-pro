The Science Behind 45 Seconds

45 seconds is a scientifically optimized duration for exercise performance. During this time frame, your body primarily uses the ATP-CP (phosphocreatine) energy system, which provides maximum power output. This makes 45-second intervals ideal for strength and power-focused training.

Research in the Journal of Strength and Conditioning shows that 45-second work intervals with 15-second rest periods create an optimal 3:1 work-to-rest ratio. This format elevates heart rate to 80-90% of maximum, triggering significant EPOC (excess post-exercise oxygen consumption) for enhanced calorie burning even after the workout ends.
